<p><p>Induction hobs, also known as induction cooktops, are kitchen appliances that use electromagnetic energy to heat cookware directly. Unlike traditional gas or electric stovetops, induction hobs do not generate heat themselves but create a magnetic field that induces heat in the cookware placed on top. This technology allows for faster and more efficient cooking, as the heat is only generated in the pans, resulting in reduced energy consumption.</p><p>The current outlook of the induction hobs market is positive and shows promising growth prospects.
