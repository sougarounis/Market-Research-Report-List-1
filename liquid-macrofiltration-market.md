<p><p>Liquid macrofiltration refers to the process of removing large particles from liquids. The market includes various types of filters such as granular micro filters, filter press filters, leaf tubular and press filters, bag filters, drum and disk filters, belt filter presses, and AWB filters. Granular micro filters use a bed of granules to filter out particles. Filter press filters involve applying pressure to separate solids from liquids. Leaf tubular and press filters use a combination of mesh screens and pressure to filter liquids. Bag filters trap impurities within a bag-like structure. Drum and disk filters utilize a rotating drum or disk to separate solids. Belt filter presses use a continuous process to filter liquids. AWB filters employ a combination of mesh screens and centrifugal force to remove impurities.</p></p>
