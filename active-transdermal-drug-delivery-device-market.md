<p><p>The active transdermal drug delivery device market includes patches and gels. Patches are small, adhesive devices that deliver medication through the skin. They are convenient and provide continuous drug release. Gels, on the other hand, are semisolid formulations applied to the skin. They are absorbed quickly and deliver drugs locally. Both patches and gels have gained popularity due to their non-invasive nature, ease of use, and ability to provide controlled drug release. These devices offer a promising alternative to traditional drug delivery methods such as oral administration or injections.</p></p>
