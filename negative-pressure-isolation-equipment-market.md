<p><p>Negative pressure isolation equipment is used to contain and control infectious agents, including airborne pathogens. There are two main types: transport isolation and ground isolation. Transport isolation equipment is designed for use in ambulances, aircraft, or other vehicles to safely transport infected patients. It includes features such as air filtration systems and sealed compartments. Ground isolation equipment is used in healthcare facilities to create negative pressure rooms, ensuring that contaminated air does not escape to other areas. These rooms are equipped with ventilation systems that draw air inward, preventing the spread of infectious agents.</p></p>
