<p><p>Fused deposition modeling (FDM) 3D printing, also known as fused filament fabrication (FFF), is a popular additive manufacturing technique that involves the process of building objects layer by layer using a thermoplastic material. It works by extruding melted plastic filament through a nozzle which is controlled by a computer-aided design (CAD) software. FDM 3D printing is widely used in various industries including aerospace, automotive, healthcare, and consumer goods due to its cost-effectiveness, versatility, and ability to produce functional prototypes.</p><p>The FDM 3D printing market has been witnessing significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 10.20% during the forecast period. The market growth can be attributed to several factors such as the increasing adoption of 3D printing technology in various industries, advancements in FDM technology, and the growing demand for customized and lightweight products. Additionally, the rising investments and initiatives by governments and private organizations are further fueling the market growth.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the FDM 3D printing market is the development of high-performance thermoplastics for FDM printers. Manufacturers are focusing on enhancing the material properties of thermoplastics such as strength, flexibility, and heat resistance to broaden the application areas of FDM 3D printing. Furthermore, the integration of FDM 3D printers with advanced software solutions for better control and optimization of the printing process is also gaining traction in the market.</p><p>In conclusion, the FDM 3D printing market is witnessing steady growth and is expected to continue expanding in the coming years. Advancements in technology, increasing adoption across industries, and the development of innovative materials and software solutions are the key drivers shaping the market's growth trajectory.</p></p>
