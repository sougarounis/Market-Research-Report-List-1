<p><p>Chip packaging refers to the process of enclosing integrated circuits (ICs) in a protective case or package. The main purpose of chip packaging is to protect the delicate circuitry from external factors such as moisture, dust, and mechanical damage, while also acting as a medium for conducting electrical signals between the chip and the external environment. It also helps in dissipating heat generated by the chip during operation.</p><p>The chip packaging market has experienced significant growth in recent years, driven by the increasing demand for compact and portable electronic devices such as smartphones, tablets, and wearable devices. <p><ul><li>Traditional Packaging</li><li>Advanced Packaging</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Chip packaging refers to the process of enclosing integrated circuits (chips) to protect them and facilitate their installation on various electronic devices. Traditional packaging is based on the use of leaded packages, such as Dual In-line Packages (DIPs), while advanced packaging employs more innovative techniques like System in Package (SiP) and Wafer Level Packaging (WLP). Traditional packaging is simple, cost-effective, and suitable for low-density chips, while advanced packaging offers higher integration, miniaturization, and performance, making it ideal for high-density and complex chips used in modern electronics.</p></p>
