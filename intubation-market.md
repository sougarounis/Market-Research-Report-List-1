<p><p>Intubation is a medical procedure that involves the insertion of a flexible tube into a patient's airway to assist with breathing or to administer anesthesia. This tube, known as an endotracheal tube, is usually placed through the mouth or nose and extends down into the trachea. Intubation is commonly used in emergency situations, surgeries, and intensive care units to provide mechanical ventilation and protect the airway.</p><p>The global Intubation Market has been experiencing significant growth and is expected to continue expanding at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 11.3% during the forecast period. Several factors contribute to the market's growth, including the rise in chronic respiratory diseases, an increase in the number of surgical procedures, and advancements in endoscopic techniques.</p><p>The prevalence of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), asthma, and other respiratory disorders has increased globally. This has resulted in a higher demand for intubation procedures to maintain adequate ventilation and manage respiratory distress. These advancements have fueled the adoption of intubation procedures across various healthcare settings.</p><p>In conclusion, the global Intubation Market is witnessing robust growth, primarily driven by the increasing prevalence of respiratory diseases and the advancements in endoscopic techniques.
