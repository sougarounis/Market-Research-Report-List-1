<p><p>A backside-illuminated (BSI) CMOS sensor is a type of image sensor that enables increased light sensitivity by reversing the traditional arrangement of sensor components. In a BSI sensor, the light-sensitive elements are placed on the backside of the sensor, allowing more light to reach the photodiodes and improving overall image quality, especially in low-light conditions. This technology has gained significant popularity in recent years, especially in digital cameras and smartphones, as it enhances image quality and sharpness.</p><p>The future outlook of the BSI CMOS sensor market is promising, as industries continue to demand improved image sensor technology for various applications. Furthermore, the automotive industry is also actively incorporating BSI CMOS sensors for applications such as advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) and autonomous vehicles.</p><p>In terms of market trends, the key focus areas include improving pixel size and resolution, enhancing image stabilization techniques, increasing sensor efficiency, and reducing power consumption. <p><p>Backside-illuminated (BSI) CMOS sensor market consists of two types: Passive Pixel Sensor CMOS and Active Pixel Sensor CMOS. Passive Pixel Sensor CMOS utilizes photodiodes for capturing light, converting it into electrical signals. These signals are then amplified and read. Active Pixel Sensor CMOS, on the other hand, uses additional circuitry in each pixel to amplify, convert, and process the light signals. Both types of sensors are widely used in various applications such as smartphones, digital cameras, and medical imaging devices, improving image quality and low-light performance.</p></p>
