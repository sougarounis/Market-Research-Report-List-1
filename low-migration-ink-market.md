<p><p>Low migration inks are designed for packaging applications to ensure minimal migration of harmful substances into the packaged product, thereby complying with strict regulations. Gravure printing uses engraved cylinders, providing excellent print quality but high costs. Flexography utilizes flexible plates and is versatile, cost-effective, and widely used. Offset printing employs transferring the ink from a plate to a rubber surface and then to the substrate. Digital printing offers flexibility, fast turnaround, and customization. Each printing method requires specific low migration ink formulations to meet regulatory requirements and ensure consumer safety.</p></p>
