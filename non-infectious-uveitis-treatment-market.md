<p><p>Non-infectious uveitis is an inflammatory condition that affects the uvea, also known as the middle layer of the eye. This condition can lead to vision loss if left untreated. Non-infectious uveitis treatment aims to reduce inflammation, relieve symptoms, and prevent complications.</p><p>Corticosteroids are commonly used in non-infectious uveitis treatment to reduce inflammation. They can be administered through eye drops, injections, or oral medications. However, long-term use of corticosteroids can have side effects, such as increased intraocular pressure and cataract formation.</p><p>Immunosuppressive drugs are also prescribed to manage non-infectious uveitis. These drugs help to control the overactive immune response that causes inflammation in the eye. Biologic agents, such as tumor necrosis factor (TNF) inhibitors, have shown promise in treating non-infectious uveitis by targeting specific immune system components.</p><p>In recent years, there has been a growing focus on developing targeted therapies for non-infectious uveitis treatment. These therapies aim to selectively block molecules responsible for inflammation and reduce the risk of side effects associated with systemic immunosuppression. Additionally, advancements in drug delivery systems, such as sustained-release implants, have improved treatment outcomes and reduced the need for frequent injections.</p><p>The non-infectious uveitis treatment market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12% during the forecast period.
