<p><p>Passive Optical LAN (POL) is a network architecture that utilizes fiber optic cables to transmit data, voice, and video signals. Two common types of POL markets are GPON (Gigabit Passive Optical Network) and EPON (Ethernet Passive Optical Network). GPON uses passive splitters to distribute signals to multiple users, providing higher bandwidth and longer reach. On the other hand, EPON uses Ethernet technology to deliver data over its network, offering lower equipment costs and simpler integration. Both GPON and EPON are popular choices for implementing POL solutions in various industries.</p></p>
