<p><p>Image-guided Surgery (IGS) Systems are advanced medical technologies that provide real-time visual guidance to surgeons during surgical procedures. There are two main types of IGS systems on the market: ones designed for open surgery and ones for endoscopy procedures. Open surgery IGS systems utilize imaging techniques such as CT scans or MRI to generate three-dimensional intraoperative maps that assist surgeons in accurately navigating and performing complex surgeries. On the other hand, endoscopy IGS systems use imaging tools, such as specialized cameras or scopes, to provide real-time visual guidance during minimally invasive procedures, allowing surgeons to navigate and manipulate surgical instruments internally.</p></p>
