<p><p>Ischemic hepatitis, also known as shock liver, is a condition characterized by acute liver injury caused by reduced blood flow to the liver due to insufficient blood perfusion. This may involve addressing conditions such as shock, heart failure, or low blood pressure, among others. Fluid resuscitation and restoration of blood flow to the liver are commonly performed to improve the overall liver function.
