<p><p>Hemodynamic monitoring systems are devices used to continuously measure the cardiovascular function of patients. The market is divided into two main types: minimally invasive and non-invasive systems. Minimally invasive systems involve the insertion of a catheter into a blood vessel to directly measure various parameters. Non-invasive systems, on the other hand, employ external sensors or cuffs to estimate the patient's blood flow and pressure without penetration. Both types provide valuable data to healthcare professionals, enabling them to monitor and manage patient's hemodynamic status effectively.</p></p>
