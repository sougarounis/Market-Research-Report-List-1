<p><p>A digital substation refers to an advanced electrical substation that incorporates digital communication technologies and intelligent electronic devices (IEDs) for better monitoring, control, and management of power systems. The increasing demand for electricity, coupled with the need for better grid management and infrastructure modernization, is driving the market growth. Digital substations offer benefits such as enhanced safety, reduced maintenance costs, improved fault detection, and increased grid reliability, which further drive their adoption.</p><p>The market is also experiencing several trends that are shaping its growth. These include the deployment of advanced sensors and monitoring systems for real-time data analysis, the integration of digital substations with cloud computing and big data analytics, and the convergence of operational technology (OT) and information technology (IT).</p><p>Additionally, the implementation of Internet of Things (IoT) technology in digital substations enables remote monitoring and control, predictive maintenance, and automation.
