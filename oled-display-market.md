<p><p>The OLED display market consists of two main types: Active-Matrix Organic Light-Emitting Diode (AMOLED) and Passive Matrix OLED (PMOLED). AMOLED displays use a matrix of thin-film transistors to control each individual pixel, offering high-resolution and vibrant colors. They are commonly found in smartphones and high-end TVs. On the other hand, PMOLED displays use a simpler matrix design, resulting in lower resolution and limited color options. They are typically used in smaller devices like smartwatches and MP3 players due to their cost-effectiveness and lower power consumption.</p></p>
