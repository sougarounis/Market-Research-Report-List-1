<p><p>A surgical adhesion barrier is a medical device used to prevent the formation of adhesions during surgical procedures. Adhesions are fibrous bands that can form between tissues and organs after surgery, causing pain, bowel obstruction, and other complications. Adhesion barriers are usually made of biocompatible materials and can be applied as gels, films, or meshes.</p><p>The global surgical adhesion barrier market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. Factors contributing to this growth include the increasing number of surgical procedures, rising geriatric population, and growing awareness about the benefits of using surgical adhesion barriers. Additionally, the advancements in technology and the introduction of innovative products are also driving market growth.</p><p>The market is also being propelled by the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases such as cardiovascular diseases, gastrointestinal disorders, and gynecological conditions, which require surgical interventions. Moreover, the rising healthcare expenditure in developing economies and the improving healthcare infrastructure are likely to contribute to market growth.</p><p>In terms of trends, the market is witnessing a shift towards the development of bio-absorbable adhesion barriers, which eliminate the need for a second surgical intervention for removal. There is also a growing preference for minimally invasive surgical procedures, which require the use of surgical adhesion barriers to minimize the risk of adhesion formation.</p><p>Overall, the surgical adhesion barrier market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 8.70% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to the increasing demand for surgical interventions and the rising awareness about the risks associated with post-surgical adhesions.</p></p>

<p><p>The surgical adhesion barrier market consists of three types: film formulation, gel formulation, and liquid formulation. Film formulation refers to a thin membrane-like barrier that is placed between tissues to prevent adhesion formation during surgeries. Gel formulation involves the application of a gel-like substance that provides a protective layer between tissues. Liquid formulation refers to a liquid solution that is sprayed or irrigated onto tissues to prevent adhesions. These different formulations aim to reduce the risk of adhesion formation and improve patient outcomes after surgery.</p></p>
