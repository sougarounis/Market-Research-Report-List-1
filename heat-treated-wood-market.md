<p><p>Heat-treated wood, also known as thermally modified wood, is a type of timber that has undergone a controlled heating process to improve its durability, strength, and resistance to decay. This process results in wood with reduced moisture content, increased dimensional stability, and improved resistance to insects, fungi, and moisture.</p><p>The heat-treated wood market is expected to experience significant growth during the forecast period. <p><p>The heat-treated wood market can be classified into two types based on the type of wood used – hardwoods and softwoods. Hardwoods come from deciduous trees, such as oak, mahogany, and walnut, and they are known for their strength, durability, and high-density. Softwoods, on the other hand, are obtained from coniferous trees, like pine, spruce, and cedar, and they are characterized by their lightweight nature and relatively lower density. Both types of wood can undergo the heat treatment process to enhance their resistance to rot, insects, and decay, making them suitable for various applications in construction and furniture industries.</p></p>
