<p><ul><li>Screw Actuators</li><li>Cam Actuators</li><li>Wheel and Axle Actuators</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Mechanical linear actuators are devices that convert rotational motion into linear motion. Screw actuators work by using a threaded screw and nut mechanism to produce linear motion. Cam actuators use a rotating cam to push or pull a linear rod. Wheel and axle actuators involve a rotating wheel that is attached to a linear rod, producing linear motion. Each of these actuator types has different applications based on their design and functionality, providing various options for mechanical linear motion control in different industries.</p></p>
