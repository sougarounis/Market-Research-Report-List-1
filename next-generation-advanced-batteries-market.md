Lithium sulfur batteries use sulfur as the cathode material, offering high energy density. Magnesium ion batteries utilize magnesium ions instead of lithium ions, providing improved safety and higher energy storage capacity. Solid electrodes batteries employ solid materials instead of liquid or gel electrolytes, enhancing stability and longevity. Metal-air batteries utilize metal as an anode and oxygen from the air as the cathode, delivering high energy density. Ultracapacitors offer rapid energy storage and release due to their high power density.</p></p>
