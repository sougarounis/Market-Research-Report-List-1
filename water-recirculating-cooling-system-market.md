<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Water recirculating cooling systems are used to regulate the temperature of industrial and commercial processes. Open recirculating cooling systems continuously recirculate water through a cooling tower, which allows for heat to be dissipated into the atmosphere. In contrast, closed recirculating cooling systems use a heat exchanger to transfer heat between the process and a closed loop of fluid. This closed loop prevents water from being lost through evaporation and reduces the risk of contamination, making it more suitable for applications where water conservation is a priority.</p></p>
