<p><ul><li>CPAP</li><li>BiPAP</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Sleep apnoea treatment devices market types include CPAP (continuous positive airway pressure) and BiPAP (bilevel positive airway pressure). CPAP machines deliver a continuous stream of air pressure to keep the airway open during sleep, treating obstructive sleep apnoea. BiPAP machines provide two different air pressure levels, higher during inhalation and lower during exhalation, making it suitable for users with more complex needs, such as those with central sleep apnoea or other respiratory conditions. Both devices are non-invasive and intended to improve breathing during sleep, reducing symptoms and improving sleep quality.</p></p>

<p><p>Sleep apnoea treatment devices are used to manage Obstructive Sleep Apnoea (OSA), Central Sleep Apnoea (CSA), and Mixed Sleep Apnoea (MSA). OSA occurs when the airway is blocked during sleep, CSA when the brain fails to signal breathing muscles, and MSA refers to a combination of both. These devices, such as Continuous Positive Airway Pressure (CPAP) machines, provide pressurized air to keep the airway open, ensuring consistent breathing throughout the night. These treatments help improve sleep quality and reduce symptoms, such as daytime fatigue and snoring, for individuals with sleep apnoea.</p></p>
