<p><p>Whole slide imaging (WSI) scanners are widely used in digital pathology to convert glass slides into digital images for analysis and interpretation. The market for WSI scanners can be categorized into two major types: Brightfield and Fluorescence. Brightfield scanners capture images using transmitted light, enabling the visualization of tissue structures and cells. Fluorescence scanners, on the other hand, employ fluorescent dyes to label specific molecules, allowing the detection of cellular components. Both types of scanners play crucial roles in various medical and research applications, facilitating precise and comprehensive analysis of tissue samples.</p></p>
