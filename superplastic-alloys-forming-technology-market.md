<p><p>Superplastic alloys forming technology refers to the process of shaping materials with exceptional deformability, allowing for complex shapes to be created. There are two main market types within this technology. The first is microstructural superplasticity, which involves manipulating the material's microstructure to enhance its ability to deform at high temperatures. The second is dynamic superplasticity, which relies on the material's ability to deform under specific strain rates. Both market types offer unique advantages and are used in various industries such as aerospace and automotive manufacturing.</p></p>
