<p><p>The remote temperature sensor market is divided into two types: wireless and wired. Wireless remote temperature sensors use radio frequency signals to transmit temperature data to a central monitoring unit, allowing for easy installation and flexibility in sensor placement. Wired remote temperature sensors, on the other hand, require physical connections to transmit temperature data, providing a reliable data transmission but with limited flexibility in sensor placement. Both types cater to different needs and preferences of customers in monitoring and controlling temperature levels remotely.</p></p>
