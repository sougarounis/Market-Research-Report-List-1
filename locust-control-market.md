<p><p>Locust control refers to the measures taken to manage and prevent the outbreak of locusts, a type of grasshopper that forms swarms and poses a significant threat to agriculture and food security. <p><p>The locust control market is categorized into four main types. Insecticide-based control involves using chemical sprays or dusts to kill locusts and halt their infestation. Biological control focuses on using natural enemies of locusts, such as predators or parasites, to control their population. Drone technology is an emerging method that uses unmanned aerial vehicles equipped with sensors and spraying systems to target locust breeding sites and effectively apply insecticides. Lastly, "others" refer to alternative control methods like trapping, pheromone-based tactics, or genetic approaches that are being explored for locust control.</p></p>
