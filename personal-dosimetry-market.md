<p><p>Personal dosimetry is the process of measuring and monitoring the radiation exposure of individuals. There are different types of dosimeters available in the market. Thermoluminescent Dosimeters (TLD) use phosphors to detect radiation, Optically Stimulated Luminescence (OSL) dosimeters utilize special crystals, and Radiophotoluminescent (RPL) dosimeters use a combination of phosphors and light. These types are considered passive dosimeters as they do not need external power. On the other hand, Active Type dosimeters have built-in electronics and real-time monitoring capabilities, allowing for immediate radiation detection and alerts. The market for personal dosimetry equipment includes all these types catering to different user needs and preferences.</p></p>
