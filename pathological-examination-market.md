<p><p>Pathological examination plays a crucial role in diagnosing diseases and determining appropriate treatments. Histopathology is the study of abnormal tissues under a microscope, while cytopathology focuses on the study of individual cells. Immunohistochemical pathology involves using antibodies to detect specific proteins or antigens in tissues, aiding in diagnosis. Molecular pathology analyzes DNA, RNA, and proteins to identify genetic mutations and abnormalities. These different types of pathology contribute to a comprehensive understanding of diseases, enabling accurate diagnosis and personalized treatment plans for patients.</p></p>
