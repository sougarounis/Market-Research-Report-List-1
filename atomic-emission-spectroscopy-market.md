<p><p>Atomic emission spectroscopy is a technique used to analyze the chemical composition of a sample by measuring the emission of light from excited atoms. This analysis can be carried out using different types of sources, including flame, spark, arc, inductively coupled plasma, and others. Flame atomic emission spectroscopy uses a flame as the excitation source, spark atomic emission spectroscopy utilizes a high-voltage spark to excite the atoms, arc atomic emission spectroscopy employs an electric arc to generate the necessary excitation, and inductively coupled plasma atomic emission spectroscopy utilizes a plasma torch to excite the atoms. Other sources may include glow discharge and laser-induced breakdown spectroscopy.</p></p>
