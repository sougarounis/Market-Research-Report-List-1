<p><p>Electrical fuses are safety devices that protect electrical circuits and equipment from overcurrent issues and short circuits by interrupting the electrical flow. These fuses consist of a metal wire or strip that melts when excessive current passes through it, thereby breaking the circuit and preventing damage to electrical components. They are widely used in various applications across industries such as residential, commercial, industrial, and automotive.</p><p>The electrical fuses market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. <p><p>The electrical fuses market consists of two main types: low voltage fuses and high voltage fuses. Low voltage fuses include plug-in fuses and cartridge fuses, which are designed to protect electrical circuits in residential, commercial, and industrial applications. Plug-in fuses are easily replaced by plugging into a socket, while cartridge fuses require a fuse holder for installation. On the other hand, high voltage fuses are specifically designed to protect electrical systems operating at high voltages, such as in power plants or electrical substations. These fuses are larger in size and provide protection against overcurrent or short circuit faults.</p></p>
