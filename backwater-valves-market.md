<p><p>Backwater valves are designed to prevent the backflow of wastewater into buildings during heavy rainfall or flooding events. Two common types of backwater valves are normally open and normally closed valves. Normally open backwater valves allow wastewater to freely flow out of a building and only close when water begins to backflow. Normally closed backwater valves remain shut and only open when there is a need for wastewater to be released. Both types play a crucial role in protecting buildings from potential water damage and contamination.</p></p>
