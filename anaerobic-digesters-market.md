<p><p>Anaerobic digesters are used for the treatment of organic waste, converting it into biogas and nutrient-rich digestate. There are several types of digesters based on their design and operation. Upflow Anaerobic Sludge Blanket (UASB) digesters use a combination of sludge retention and gas-liquid-solid separation. Internal Circulation (IC) Reactor digesters are equipped with internals to enhance the internal circulation of the biomass. Expanded Granular Sludge Bed (EGSB) digesters have granular biomass and operate at higher loading rates. Other digesters include plug-flow, complete mix, and fixed-film digesters, each designed to suit specific waste characteristics and treatment objectives.</p></p>
