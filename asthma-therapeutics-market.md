<p><p>Asthma therapeutics refers to the medications and treatment options that are used to manage and control asthma, a chronic respiratory condition characterized by inflammation and narrowing of the airways. Biologics are drugs derived from living organisms that target specific components of the immune system responsible for the inflammation in asthma. These therapies, such as monoclonal antibodies, have shown promising results in reducing asthma exacerbations and improving lung function in patients with severe asthma.</p><p>Another trend in the market is the shift towards personalized medicine. With advancements in genetic testing and understanding of asthma subtypes, healthcare providers can tailor therapies to individual patients based on their specific characteristics and needs.
